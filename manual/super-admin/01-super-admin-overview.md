# Super Admin Overview

## Purpose of the Super Admin role
The **Super Admin** has full, system‑wide visibility and control across all companies, vendors, and operational data. This role is responsible for the overall configuration of the platform and for managing who can access what.

## Where you work
You will work in the **Admin Panel** (the Filament admin interface). Your team will provide the exact URL for your environment.

## Main navigation areas
The left‑hand menu is grouped by functional areas. As a Super Admin, you can access all of them.

### Orders
- **Service Orders**: view, create, assign, and track operational work orders.

### Customers
- **Customers**: manage customer accounts and details.
- **Agents**: manage customer‑side agents/contacts.
- **Site Equipment**: manage equipment tied to customer sites.

### Service Partners
- **Vendors**: manage vendor records.
- **Vendor Companies**: manage vendor organizations.
- **Vendor Branches**: manage vendor branches/locations.

### Technicians
- **Technicians**: manage technicians and their profiles.
- **Technician Teams**: group technicians into teams.

### Pricing Table
- **Pricing Tables**: manage master pricing tables.
- **Catalog Items**: manage catalog items tied to pricing.
- **Pricing Table Overrides**: set exceptions or overrides to standard pricing.

### Settings
- **Users**: create and manage user accounts and roles.
- **Regions**: manage geographic regions used throughout the system.

## What a Super Admin can do
- **Global access**: see all records across vendors, customers, and companies.
- **User management**: create users and assign roles (Super Admin, Admin, Vendor Admin, Vendor Staff).
- **Platform configuration**: manage regions and core pricing structures.
- **Operational oversight**: view and manage service orders, technicians, and customer data.

## Recommended workflows
1. **Set up core configuration**
   - Create Regions in **Settings → Regions**.
   - Build Pricing Tables and Catalog Items under **Pricing Table**.

2. **Onboard service partners**
   - Add Vendors, Vendor Companies, and Vendor Branches under **Service Partners**.

3. **Set up users**
   - Create user accounts in **Settings → Users**.
   - Assign the correct role based on responsibilities.

4. **Manage operations**
   - Use **Orders → Service Orders** to create, assign, and track work.
   - Maintain technicians and teams under **Technicians**.

## Tips for working efficiently
- Use table filters and search to find records quickly.
- Use bulk actions carefully; they apply to all selected records.
- Keep naming consistent across vendors, branches, and pricing items to avoid confusion.

## Need help?
If you are unsure which area to use or see missing access, contact your system administrator or implementation team.
